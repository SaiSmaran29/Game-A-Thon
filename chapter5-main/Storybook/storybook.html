Detective DeskTHE MIDNIGHT MURDER

Vision Night was supposed to be Arjun Varma's finest hour.

At thirty-four, he had built Veyra Technologies from a dorm-room idea into a company worth watching — and tonight, he would unveil Project Solace to the people who mattered most.

He had opened Twilight Cove, his private lake house, for the celebration. Soft light on the water. Champagne already chilling. Four people he trusted enough to invite early, before the rest of the world arrived.

Meera Kapoor. Rohan Mehta. Ishita Rao. Devika Singh.

By midnight, only one of them would still believe he deserved any of it.

At 11:47 PM, the lights at Twilight Cove went out.

No one inside the house called for help.

By the time someone did, Arjun Varma was already gone.

The lake was still. The celebration never started.

Four guests. One body. No sign of who had been the last to leave the room.

Vision Night would never happen — not the way anyone had planned.

No corrections came from inside the house. No one had seen anything before the lights died.

The investigation began with four names, equally distant from innocence.

Detective work doesn't wait for shock to pass. The questions started before sunrise.

Then, slowly, the same four stories began to bend.

Alibis arrived late.

Timelines missed their windows by minutes that mattered.

Meera Kapoor's hands trembled when she mentioned the company she helped build — a company that, on paper, no longer listed her name.

Rohan Mehta's eyes flicked toward the door whenever Vision Night came up, tracking an exit that wasn't there.

Ishita Rao smiled through every question about Arjun, a smile that arrived a half-second after it should have.

Devika Singh recited her schedule too perfectly, the way people do when they've rehearsed it.

No single detail explained anything. No one had a visible motive — not on the surface.

They each had a reason to want him gone. So did the truth, eventually.

Then an anonymous tip surfaced — pointing investigators toward an outsider.

Wrong name. Wrong moment.

Detectives chased it anyway, because ignoring a lead in a murder case costs more than time.

It cost them worse.

The trail dissolved within hours. Whoever planted it knew exactly how long the distraction would hold.

One inconsistency finally broke the silence.

A timestamp. A saved post. A deleted comment that should never have existed.

Three stories held. One did not.

The pieces scattered across four lives began to move toward a single point.

THE TRUTH STRUCK BACK.

This time, nothing could hold it back.

Scattered across four digital lives were three threads no one had connected.

A location no one was supposed to know.

A memory no one was supposed to remember.

A weapon no one was supposed to recognize.

Each had an explanation alone.

Together, they pointed to Twilight Cove, a six-year-old promise, and a motive buried under a company's growing success.

Vision Night had only four guests.

Only one of them walked out with everything to lose.

This was not a tragedy that happened in the dark. It was a murder that hid in plain sight.

There is a killer among them.
